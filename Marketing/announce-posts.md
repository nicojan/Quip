# Quip — launch copy

## Reddit — r/MacOSApps

**Title:**

Quip: a tiny macOS menu-bar app for finding a GIF and pasting it anywhere (MIT, bring your own Giphy key)

**Body:**

Grabbing a GIF for a chat always cost me four steps: open a browser tab, search Giphy, save the file, drag it in. So I built Quip, a free menu-bar app that cuts it to three: search, click, paste.

Quip lives in the menu bar. You search Giphy, click a result, and it lands on your clipboard as an animated file ready to paste into Messages, Slack, or anywhere that takes an image.

What it does:

- Search Giphy from the menu bar and click any GIF to copy it. No downloads, no drag-and-drop.
- Star a GIF to keep it one click away in Favorites.
- Group favorites into named collections, each with its own emoji.
- Recently copied keeps the GIFs you actually used within reach, and recent searches let you re-run a query with one click.
- Three sizes: two, three, or five GIFs per row, whichever fits your screen.
- Start at login, and in-app updates through Sparkle.

One catch worth naming: Quip ships without an API key. You add your own free key from the Giphy dashboard, which takes a minute. The upside is that you're never sharing someone else's rate limit, and the key stays on your Mac.

It runs on macOS 14.6 and up. The build is signed, notarized, and updates itself through Sparkle. Written in Swift.
